  • 02

    Burn Fat — Incline treadmill finishers + compound-heavy training to elevate metabolism
  • 04
    Improve Cardio — Low-impact incline walking 3–4× per week, increasing grade over 8 weeks
Split Structure
Upper / Lower Split — Best choice for your profile. Allows each muscle group 2× weekly frequency for muscle growth, keeps any single session under 60 min, and lets us isolate lower-body days to be fully back-safe with hip/glute emphasis while upper days handle the heavier pressing and pulling.

Day Structure: Upper A → Lower A → Rest → Upper B → Lower B → Optional Cardio/Active → Rest

Phase Summaries

Phase 1 — Weeks 1–3: Foundation & Form

Establish movement patterns, back-safe hip hinge mechanics, and baseline volume. Moderate loads, strict tempo.

  • Sets: 3 per exercise | Reps: 10–12 | Rest: 75 sec
  • Incline walk: 10 min at 6–8% grade, 3.0 mph
  • Focus: Perfect form, feel the right muscles working

Phase 2 — Weeks 4–6: Hypertrophy Peak

Increase volume and load. This is the primary muscle-building block. Tempos tighten, weights go up.

  • Sets: 4 per exercise | Reps: 8–10 | Rest: 60–90 sec
  • Incline walk: 12–15 min at 8–10% grade, 3.2 mph
  • Focus: Progressive overload, controlled fatigue

Phase 3 — Weeks 7–8: Intensification

Maintain volume, push heavier loads, introduce drop sets on key exercises. Peak performance block.

  • Sets: 4–5 | Reps: 6–8 (heavy) | Rest: 90 sec
  • Incline walk: 15 min at 10–12% grade, 3.3 mph
  • Focus: Maximum load, strength expression

Progressive Overload Rules

When to Add Weight

  • Complete all reps of all sets with good form
  • The last rep of the last set feels like RPE 7–8 (not 10)
  • Add 2.5–5 lbs next session for that exercise
  • On pull-ups: when you hit the top of the rep range cleanly, add a rep next week

When to Scale Back

  • Form breaks down before completing all reps — drop weight 10%
  • Any lower back sensation — reduce range of motion or swap exercise
  • Fatigue carryover from the previous day — reduce sets to 2
  • Soreness so bad you can't move well — take an extra rest day

Treadmill Progression

  • Weeks 1–2: 6–8% grade, 3.0 mph, 10 min
  • Weeks 3–4: 8–10% grade, 3.0 mph, 12 min
  • Weeks 5–6: 10% grade, 3.2 mph, 15 min
  • Weeks 7–8: 12% grade, 3.2–3.5 mph, 15 min

Pull-Up Progression

  • Wk 1–2: 3 × 6–8 reps (bodyweight)
  • Wk 3–4: 3 × 8–10 reps
  • Wk 5–6: 4 × 8–10 reps
  • Wk 7–8: 4 × 10–12 or add band-weight vest if available
Week-by-Week Blueprint

Weeks 1–3: Foundation

Base template. Focus on form, not weight. Learn the hip thrust pattern — this is your deadlift replacement.

  • Sets: 3 per main exercise
  • Reps: 10–12 (upper), 10–12 (lower)
  • Rest: 75 sec upper, 90 sec lower
  • Cardio: 10–12 min at 6–8% grade
  • Week 3: increase all loads by 5 lbs where form is clean

Weeks 4–6: Hypertrophy

Add 1 set to every main exercise. Reduce rest periods by 15 sec. Increase treadmill grade and duration.

  • Sets: 4 per main exercise
  • Reps: 8–10 (heavier loads)
  • Rest: 60–75 sec upper, 75–90 sec lower
  • Cardio: 12–15 min at 8–10% grade
  • Week 5: add tempo technique — increase lowering phase to 4 seconds on one exercise per session
  • Week 6: introduce 1 drop set per session (last set of heaviest exercise, drop 20%, continue to failure)

Weeks 7–8: Intensification

Max effort block. Push loads on all main lifts. Keep form strict.

  • Sets: 4–5 per main exercise
  • Reps: 6–8 (at highest sustainable load)
  • Rest: 90 sec throughout
  • Cardio: 15 min at 10–12% grade
  • Week 7: drop sets on hip thrust AND bench press
  • Week 8: test your 1-rep-range max on major lifts — note for cycle 2

Safety Guidelines & Form Cues

Lower Back Protocol

  • No conventional deadlifts for the full 8 weeks
  • RDL kept light (20–30 lbs) and range limited to mid-shin
  • All rowing kept at 45° torso angle maximum
  • Core braced before EVERY single rep of every exercise
  • If back tightens during a set — rack the weight, reassess
  • Red flag: sharp, shooting, or radiating pain = stop immediately

Hip Thrust (Key Lift)

  • Upper back across the bench, not neck or lower
  • Feet flat, heels directly under knees
  • Drive through entire foot — not toes
  • Chin tucked neutral — don't crane neck back
  • Top position: straight line from shoulder to knee
  • Progress: master bodyweight → add DB → increase load

Shoulder Safety

  • Always do band pull-aparts before pressing
  • On shoulder press: do NOT fully lock out overhead
  • Lateral raises: slight forward lean, thumbs pointing slightly down
  • Face pulls every upper day — non-negotiable for shoulder health
  • If shoulder clicks or catches: reduce range of motion first

Auto-Regulation Rules

  • RPE 9–10 every session = overreaching. Add rest day.
  • RPE under 6 for 2+ sessions = increase load or add a set
  • Unusual soreness in a joint (not muscle) = rest that area
  • Missed a day? Never double up lower days back to back
  • Travel or busy week? Keep 2 sessions minimum (Upper A + Lower A)

Equipment Substitutions

  • No bench for hip thrust → Use floor glute bridge (harder to load but valid)
  • No bench for chest press → DB floor press
  • Nordic curls → Slow eccentric hamstring curls with band
  • Bulgarian split squat → Reverse lunge (more accessible)
  • Incline press without bench → Decline push-up variation

Nutrition Reminders

  • Target: ~165g protein/day (1g per lb bodyweight)
  • Pre-workout: protein + carbs 1–2 hrs before
  • Post-workout: protein within 60 min after training
  • Hydration: 3+ liters/day, extra on training days
  • Calorie deficit for fat loss: 200–300 cal under maintenance
